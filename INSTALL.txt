NANO GDPR - INSTALL
===================

Uploading the files is not the whole job. The banner only appears once you
add a few lines to your web page. The banner injects itself, so there is no
banner HTML to paste, just the three lines below.

STEP 1: UPLOAD
--------------
Put the "gdpr" folder at the top level of your website, so the files are
reachable at:

    https://yoursite.com/gdpr/

STEP 2: ADD THESE LINES TO YOUR PAGE <head>
-------------------------------------------
Paste this into the <head> of your page. If your site uses a shared header
include, put it there once so it appears on every page.

    <link rel="stylesheet" href="/gdpr/nano-gdpr.css">
    <script>
      window.NanoGDPR = {
        text: "We use cookies to improve your experience on this site.",
        privacyUrl: "/privacy-policy"
      };
    </script>
    <script src="/gdpr/nano-gdpr.js" defer></script>

Change privacyUrl to your real privacy page. That is the whole install.

STEP 3 (IMPORTANT): HOLD BACK TRACKING SCRIPTS
----------------------------------------------
A consent banner is only lawful if the cookies it asks about do not fire
until the visitor agrees. Take any analytics or marketing script, change its
type to text/plain, and tag it with a category. It then waits for consent.

    <script type="text/plain" data-nano-gdpr="analytics"
            data-src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id=G-XXXX"></script>

The script runs only after the visitor accepts the "Analytics" category.

STEP 4: A "COOKIE SETTINGS" LINK (so people can change their mind)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Put this anywhere, for example in your footer:

    <a href="#" data-nano-gdpr-open>Cookie settings</a>

THAT IS IT
----------
The banner shows on the first visit. Once a choice is made it is remembered
and will not show again. While testing, run NanoGDPR.reset() in the browser
console to clear the choice and see the banner again.

More detail is in GUIDE.html and README.md, or online at:
https://github.com/digifrac/Nano-GDPR
